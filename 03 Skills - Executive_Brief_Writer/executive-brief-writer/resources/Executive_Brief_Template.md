# Executive Brief Templates

## 1. Quick Input Check

Before drafting, identify:

| Item | Question |
|---|---|
| Audience | Who will read or decide? |
| Purpose | What must the reader understand, approve, or do? |
| Period | What dates or reporting period apply? |
| Evidence | Which facts and calculations are reliable? |
| Options | What realistic alternatives exist? |
| Recommendation | What action is proposed? |
| Risk | What could materially change the conclusion? |
| Next step | Who must do what, and by when? |

When an item is unavailable, label it as an evidence gap rather than inventing content.

---

# 2. Standard Executive Brief

## Title

Use a conclusion-led title that communicates the issue or proposed direction.

**Reporting period:** State the relevant period.

## Decision or Purpose

State in one or two sentences what leadership must understand, approve, reject, revise, or monitor.

## Executive Summary

Write a short paragraph covering:

- the main conclusion;
- why it matters now;
- the proposed management response.

## Key Findings

Present no more than five findings. For each finding:

1. state the evidence;
2. quantify it where possible;
3. explain the business implication.

## Recommendation

State the proposed action, why it is preferred, and any conditions that should apply.

## Principal Risks and Assumptions

List only the risks or assumptions that could materially change the recommendation.

## Immediate Next Steps

| Action | Accountable function | Timing | Success measure |
|---|---|---|---|

## Decision Required

State the exact approval, selection, escalation, or acknowledgement requested from leadership.

---

# 3. Decision Brief

Use this structure when leadership must choose an option.

## Recommendation

Lead with the preferred option and decision requested.

## Why the Decision Is Needed Now

Explain urgency, opportunity cost, or exposure from delay.

## Material Evidence

Summarise the facts and calculations that determine the choice.

## Options Considered

| Option | Advantages | Disadvantages | Financial or operational effect | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## Recommended Option

Explain why it is preferred and what would make another option preferable.

## Approval Conditions

State any pilot, validation, budget, governance, or review conditions.

## Immediate Actions

List the first actions after approval.

---

# 4. Performance Brief

Use this structure for KPI, financial, customer, or operational performance.

## Overall Assessment

State whether performance is on track, mixed, or off track and explain the main reason.

## Performance Summary

| Measure | Current | Target | Prior period | Status | Management implication |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|---|

## Material Drivers

Separate:

- confirmed drivers;
- probable explanations;
- information still required.

## Recommended Actions

Provide no more than five actions, ranked by impact and urgency.

## Watch Indicators

State the indicators and thresholds management should monitor.

## Decision Required

State the required decision or endorsement.

---

# 5. Issue Brief

Use this structure for an incident, problem, or emerging risk.

## Issue and Severity

Define the issue, affected area, timing, and current severity.

## What Is Known

List verified facts only.

## What Is Not Yet Known

List the most important evidence gaps.

## Business Exposure

Explain financial, customer, operational, regulatory, people, and reputational consequences where relevant.

## Immediate Containment

State the actions required now.

## Root-Cause Work

List hypotheses and the evidence needed to test them.

## Escalation or Decision Required

State who must decide what and by when.

---

# 6. Strategy Brief

Use this structure for strategy, market, growth, or transformation proposals.

## Strategic Conclusion

State the recommended direction and the reason it matters.

## Current Position

Summarise the few facts that define the starting point.

## Strategic Choice

Explain what the organisation will prioritise and what it will not prioritise.

## Value Logic

Describe how the choice creates revenue, margin, customer, capability, or risk value.

## Required Capabilities

Identify people, process, technology, partner, and governance requirements.

## Milestones and Decision Gates

List the major stages, evidence required, and conditions for continuing.

## Risks and Trade-offs

State the principal risks and what management is choosing to accept.

## Decision Required

State the exact approval requested.

---

# 7. Executive Writing Rules

- Put the conclusion before the supporting detail.
- Use a specific subject and reporting period.
- Prefer quantified statements to vague descriptions.
- Explain the implication of every major finding.
- Use no more than five main findings.
- Use tables only for comparison, not decoration.
- Keep sentences direct and avoid unnecessary jargon.
- Label assumptions and inferences.
- Do not claim a cause when the evidence shows only correlation.
- End with a clear decision or action.

---

# 8. Quality Rubric

Score each area from 1 to 5.

| Criterion | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Purpose | Unclear | Generally understandable | Exact decision or purpose is explicit |
| Evidence | Unsupported | Mostly supported | Material claims are traceable and correctly labelled |
| Prioritisation | Too much detail | Some prioritisation | Only decision-relevant content remains |
| Business implication | Mostly descriptive | Some implications | Every major finding explains why it matters |
| Recommendation | Vague or absent | Direction is present | Specific, feasible, evidence-based, and conditional where needed |
| Risk | Missing | General risks | Material risks, assumptions, and trade-offs are visible |
| Actionability | No owners or timing | Partial action plan | Clear actions, accountability, timing, and measures |
| Executive style | Difficult to scan | Acceptable | Concise, conclusion-led, and meeting-ready |

A brief should normally score at least **32 out of 40** before senior-management circulation.
