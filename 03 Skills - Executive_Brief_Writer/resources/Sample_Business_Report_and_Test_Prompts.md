# Sample Business Report and Test Prompts

## Fictional Demonstration Company

**Company:** HarborOne Services Ltd.  
**Industry:** Business facilities and workplace services  
**Reporting period:** Quarter 2, 2026  
**Audience:** Chief executive officer and executive committee

All information in this case is fictional and intended only for learning and demonstration.

---

# 1. Source Report

## Business Context

HarborOne provides facilities management, workplace support, maintenance, and cleaning services to medium-sized and large corporate customers. Management entered 2026 with two priorities:

1. grow revenue in the enterprise segment;
2. improve contract renewal and service consistency.

The company began expanding into two new cities during Quarter 2. The expansion requires additional supervisors, local suppliers, vehicles, and working capital.

## Quarter 2 Performance

| Measure | Q2 2026 actual | Q2 target | Q2 2025 actual |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Revenue | USD 24.8m | USD 25.5m | USD 23.4m |
| Gross margin | 36.2% | 38.0% | 37.5% |
| Operating profit | USD 2.1m | USD 2.8m | USD 2.6m |
| Enterprise contract renewal | 89.0% | 92.0% | 91.0% |
| On-time service completion | 94.0% | 96.0% | 95.5% |
| Customer complaints | 142 | 100 maximum | 98 |
| Employee overtime hours | 18,600 | 14,000 maximum | 15,200 |
| Strategic expansion completion | 62% | 70% | Not applicable |

## Additional Findings

- Revenue increased by USD 1.4 million compared with Q2 2025 but remained USD 0.7 million below target.
- New-city contracts generated USD 1.9 million of revenue during the quarter.
- New-city contracts delivered a gross margin of 27%, compared with 37% in established cities.
- Temporary labour and overtime added approximately USD 0.55 million above the operating plan.
- Three enterprise customers representing USD 3.2 million of annual revenue are due for renewal during Quarter 3.
- Two of those customers have formally raised concerns about delayed maintenance visits.
- Customer complaints were concentrated in the two new cities and in one established-city maintenance team.
- Management has not yet completed a customer-level profitability review for the new-city contracts.
- The expansion programme is two weeks behind schedule because supervisor recruitment and supplier onboarding took longer than planned.
- The chief operating officer believes the company should continue expansion to preserve sales momentum.
- The chief financial officer recommends pausing additional sales commitments until service delivery and contract economics improve.

## Management Decision

The executive committee must decide whether to:

1. continue the current expansion pace;
2. pause new-city sales commitments for sixty days while stabilising operations;
3. continue only selected high-margin opportunities while applying strict approval controls.

# 5. Model Executive Brief

## Stabilise Service Delivery While Continuing Only Controlled High-Margin Expansion

**Reporting period:** Quarter 2, 2026  
**Decision required:** Approve a sixty-day selective-expansion control period rather than continuing unrestricted growth or stopping all new-city activity.

### Executive Summary

HarborOne achieved year-on-year revenue growth, but the expansion is currently reducing margin, service consistency, and operating profit. Revenue rose USD 1.4 million from Q2 2025, yet remained USD 0.7 million below target. Gross margin was 1.8 percentage points below target, while operating profit was USD 0.7 million below target and USD 0.5 million below the prior year. The recommended response is to continue only opportunities that meet margin, capacity, and service-readiness criteria while management stabilises the two new cities over the next sixty days.

### Key Findings

1. **Growth quality is weak.** New-city contracts generated USD 1.9 million, but their 27% gross margin was ten percentage points below established-city performance.
2. **Delivery cost is above plan.** Temporary labour and overtime added approximately USD 0.55 million above plan, contributing to the operating-profit shortfall.
3. **Service risk is increasing.** On-time completion fell to 94% against a 96% target, while complaints increased to 142 from 98 a year earlier.
4. **Renewal exposure is material.** Three enterprise customers representing USD 3.2 million of annual revenue renew in Q3; two have reported delayed maintenance visits.
5. **Expansion readiness is incomplete.** The programme is two weeks late and only 62% complete against a 70% target because recruitment and supplier onboarding are behind plan.

### Recommendation

Adopt selective controlled expansion for sixty days. Approve only contracts that meet a minimum margin threshold, have confirmed supervisor and supplier capacity, and do not compromise the three priority renewals. This protects sales momentum while reducing the risk of locking in unprofitable work.

### Principal Risks and Assumptions

The main risks are further renewal deterioration, continued overtime dependence, and inaccurate contract economics. A customer-level profitability review is still required before management can confirm the full value of the new-city portfolio.

### Immediate Actions

| Action | Accountable function | Timing | Success measure |
|---|---|---|---|
| Complete recovery plans for the three Q3 renewals | Commercial and Operations | Within 10 business days | Renewal plans agreed; service issues closed |
| Review profitability and capacity for every new-city contract | Finance and Operations | Within 15 business days | Contract-level margin and staffing decision completed |
| Apply executive approval to new contracts during the control period | CEO, CFO, and COO | Immediate | No contract accepted below agreed margin and readiness thresholds |

### Decision Required

Approve the sixty-day selective-expansion control period, the contract approval gates, and a formal executive review at the end of the period.

# 6. Evaluation Checklist

A successful demonstration should show that Claude:

- states the recommended direction in the opening section;
- uses the supplied figures accurately;
- does not invent customer, margin, or cost data;
- explains why revenue growth has not translated into profit growth;
- recognises the Q3 renewal exposure;
- compares the management options rather than presenting only one;
- provides actions with accountability, timing, and measures;
- identifies the missing profitability review as an evidence gap;
- ends with an exact management decision;
- remains concise enough for senior leadership.
